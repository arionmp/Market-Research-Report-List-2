<p><p>Graphene flakes are thin layers of graphene that can be categorized into single-layer graphene flakes and bilayer graphene flakes. Single-layer graphene flakes consist of a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice structure, offering excellent electrical conductivity and mechanical strength. Bilayer graphene flakes, on the other hand, consist of two layers of graphene stacked on top of each other. They exhibit unique properties that differ from single-layer graphene flakes, making them suitable for various applications in the electronics and materials industries.</p></p>
