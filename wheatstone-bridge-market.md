It consists of four resistors connected in a diamond shape with a voltage source applied across two opposite corners and a galvanometer connected across the other two corners. <p><p>A Wheatstone Bridge is a type of circuit used to measure unknown electrical resistances. There are two main market types for Wheatstone Bridges: balanced bridge and unbalanced bridge markets. In a balanced bridge market, all four resistors in the circuit are equal, resulting in a zero output voltage when the bridge is balanced. In an unbalanced bridge market, the resistors are not equal, leading to a non-zero output voltage when the bridge is unbalanced. Both types of markets are used for different applications in electrical measurements.</p></p>
