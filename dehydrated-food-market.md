<p><p>Dehydrated food market types include various methods of removing moisture from food products to prolong their shelf life. Some common methods include Spray Dried, Freeze Dried, Vacuum Dried, Sun Dried, Hot Air Dried, and others. Spray drying involves spraying food with hot air to remove moisture quickly. Freeze drying involves freezing food and then removing the ice through sublimation. Vacuum drying is done under low pressure to evaporate moisture. Sun drying is a traditional method of using sunlight to dehydrate food. Hot air drying involves using heated air to remove moisture from food.</p></p>
