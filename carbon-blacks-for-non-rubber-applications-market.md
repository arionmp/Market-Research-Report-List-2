<p><p>Carbon blacks for non-rubber applications come in various types, with two common types being thermal black and furnace black. Thermal black is produced using a gas-phase combustion process, while furnace black is produced from the incomplete combustion of heavy oil feedstock. Both types exhibit high levels of thermal conductivity and UV protection, making them ideal for applications outside of the rubber industry, such as plastics, inks, coatings, and batteries. These carbon blacks offer improved performance and durability in various end-use products.</p></p>
