<p><p>Non-invasive ventilation machines like CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) and BiPAP (Bi-level Positive Airway Pressure) are both used for the treatment of breathing disorders such as sleep apnea and respiratory failure. </p><p>CPAP machines deliver a constant level of air pressure to keep the airways open during sleep, while BiPAP machines provide varying pressure levels for both inhalation and exhalation, making it easier for the user to breathe. Both types of machines are crucial in improving respiratory function and increasing oxygen levels in patients without the need for invasive procedures.</p></p>
