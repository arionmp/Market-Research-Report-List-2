<p><p>Roofing underlying materials are essential components for ensuring the durability and protection of roofs. The three main types in the market include asphalt-saturated felt, modified asphalt, and non-bitumen synthetic roofing underlying. Asphalt-saturated felt is a traditional, cost-effective option made from organic materials. Modified asphalt utilizes polymers for enhanced strength and durability. Non-bitumen synthetic underlying materials are manufactured from materials like polyethylene or polypropylene, offering lightweight and resistant solutions for modern roofing needs. Each type offers unique benefits and characteristics to suit various roofing applications.</p></p>
