<p><p>Inductive proximity sensors are a type of sensor used in industrial applications to detect the presence of metallic objects without physical contact. The two main types are NPN and PNP inductive proximity sensors. NPN sensors are active low devices where the output signal drops to 0V when an object is detected, while PNP sensors are active high devices where the output signal rises to the supply voltage when an object is detected. Both types offer reliable and accurate detection capabilities in various industrial settings.</p></p>
