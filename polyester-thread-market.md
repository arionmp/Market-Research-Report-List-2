<p><p>Polyester thread market types are categorized based on their thickness, measured in denier (D). Common types include 70D, 100D, 150D, 210D, and 250D. Each denier represents the weight in grams of 9000 meters of the thread. Threads with higher denier like 210D and 250D are thicker and stronger, suitable for heavy-duty sewing projects. On the other hand, threads with lower denier like 70D and 100D are finer and more delicate, ideal for lightweight fabrics and intricate stitching. Other denier categories may exist based on specific industry requirements.</p></p>
