<p><p>Preclinical tomography systems for biopharmaceuticals are used for non-invasive imaging to determine the effectiveness of drug candidates before clinical trials. The market types include CT (computed tomography) which uses X-rays to produce cross-sectional images, SPECT (single photon emission computed tomography) which measures gamma rays emitted by radioactive tracers, and other technologies such as PET (positron emission tomography). These imaging modalities provide valuable insights into drug distribution, metabolism, and efficacy in preclinical studies, helping to streamline the drug development process.</p></p>
