<p><p>Plant fat replacers are substances used in food products to mimic the texture and mouthfeel of fats while reducing the overall caloric content. These replacers can be classified into three main types: carbohydrate-based, protein-based, and lipid-based. Carbohydrate-based fat replacers include fiber-rich ingredients like cellulose and gums. Protein-based fat replacers use proteins like whey and soy to mimic the creamy texture of fats. Lipid-based fat replacers are made from oils or emulsifiers that provide a rich mouthfeel without the added calories of traditional fats.</p></p>
