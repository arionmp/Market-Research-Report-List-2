<p><p>Catalysts are substances that increase the rate of a chemical reaction without being consumed in the process. They can be classified into two categories: organic catalysts, which are derived from carbon-containing compounds, and inorganic catalysts, which are derived from non-carbon-containing compounds. Organic catalysts are commonly used in industries like pharmaceuticals and food processing, while inorganic catalysts are often utilized in the production of chemicals and fuels. Both types play crucial roles in speeding up reactions and reducing energy costs in various industrial processes.</p></p>
