<p><p>Hydraulic directional drilling rigs are classified into two main types based on the ground conditions they are designed to operate in: soft ground and hard rock markets. Soft ground rigs are more suitable for terrains with loose soil, sand, or clay, where stability is a concern. On the other hand, hard rock rigs are specifically engineered to drill through solid rock formations efficiently. Each type of rig is equipped with different mechanisms and components to ensure optimal performance in their respective market conditions.</p></p>
